Formula to calculate IMAX Benefit %

Shrey Tyagi • 16 April, 2025

IMAX advertises 26% extra image for their expanded aspect ratio movies. But that holds true only if the entire movie is expanded. If the movie only has 10-20% scenes expanded, is it not wrong to say 26% more? Because for 80% of the runtime you'll still be watching the exact image as non-IMAX venues. So to calculate the effective extra image, we need to consider the duration of IMAX scenes. This below formula should help which I constructed.

  • Df = Duration of Full Movie
  • Di = Duration of IMAX Scenes
  • Do = Duration of Opening Credits
  • De = Duration of End Credits
  • An = Aspect Ratio of non-IMAX Scenes
  • Ai = Aspect Ratio of IMAX Scenes

\[ \frac{D_i}{D_f - D_o - D_e} \times \left( \frac{A_n}{A_i} - 1 \right) \times 100 \]

Example for the movie Sinners which has 25 mins of IMAX footage and a runtime of 137 mins, the values will be:

  • Df = 137
  • Di = 25
  • Do = 1
  • De = 5
  • An = 2.76
  • Ai = 1.43

\[ \frac{25}{137 - 1 - 5} \times \left( \frac{2.76}{1.43} - 1 \right) \times 100 \]

= 16%
